## Context

Student robotics competition at ECAM Lyon (Engineering Degree, 2018–2022), themed around the ITER nuclear fusion project. Teams were tasked with designing and building a robot capable of autonomously collecting and sorting objects representing simulated nuclear waste.

## Approach

The control architecture was built around a **Grafcet-style state machine**: a structured, deterministic control model well-suited to sequential robotics tasks with discrete states and sensor-triggered transitions.

- **Navigation and manipulation** driven by sensor inputs (obstacle detection, object identification, positioning)
- **DC motors** for locomotion and actuation, controlled via a Raspberry Pi
- **Electrical design** handled from scratch: wiring, power distribution, motor drivers

My contributions covered the full electrical side of the robot and the entire control algorithm implementation.

## Result

The team won the competition.
